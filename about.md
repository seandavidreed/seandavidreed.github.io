Hello! I'm an instructor at Cappella Music Academy, teaching guitar, bass, and ukulele one-on-one to over 50 students on a weekly basis. June of 2023 marks four wonderful years of teaching. For me, these are the two greatest rewards of teaching, seeing the spark of excitement on a student's face when they grasp concepts, and the opportunity to become a much better learner myself.

When I'm not teaching, I'm learning Computer Science. I've been studying since September of 2021 with the aim of creating new employment opportunities for myself in the future, and in the process, I've become enamored with programming and building my own applications.

I've approached this effort by, in a sense, curating my own curriculum. Following several suggestions from friends and colleagues who have taken this path ahead of me, I've taken courses (Harvard's CS50x, C Programming); read books (Code: The Hidden Language of Computer Hardware and Software, The Art of Clean Code, The Missing Readme); watched documentaries and lectures on the history of computing (learning more about the works of giants like Von Neumann and Alan Turing); and most importantly, I've designed and implemented my own projects (a Student Log console application written in C, and an Inventory Management Web Application with Django and Python, to name a couple).

My current efforts involve expanding my knowledge of C++ features and syntax with the course C++20 Masterclass on Udemy, learning how to program microcontrollers with Bare Metal C, and planning for future courses of study. Right now, I'm considering two classes for the future: Internet of Things (IoT) from Arm Education X or Tiny Machine Learning (TinyML) from Harvard EdX. 

Thank you for reading. I'm excited for all that is to come!
